THE KINGDOM CONCEPT OF PREDESTINATION (Part 7)

Sub-Series Focus: The 3 Key Things About The Will of God (Part 1) – You Must Take Responsibility of Knowing It

TEXTS: Ephesians 5:17, Colossians 1:9-10

INTRODUCTION

Knowing God’s will is not optional; it is essential. Many believers expect God to forcefully reveal His plan, but the responsibility to seek it is ours. Even Apostle Paul withdrew to Arabia for years to understand his divine assignment (Galatians 1:15-18). Destiny is too precious to leave to guesswork—take responsibility!

TODAY’S LESSON (5 Key Points)

  1. The Will of God is Knowable but Not Automatic
    Ephesians 5:17—“Understand what the will of the Lord is.”

    God’s will is not a mystery meant to remain hidden; it is a truth waiting to be pursued. Ignorance is a choice, not a destiny.

  2. Seeking God’s Will is a Personal Duty
    Colossians 1:9—Paul prayed to be filled with knowledge of His will.

    No one can do this for you. You must invest time in the Word, prayer, and spiritual growth to uncover what God has written about you.

  3. Revelation Comes Through Separation and Pursuit
    Galatians 1:17—Paul separated himself to seek God.

    Clarity demands consecration. You can’t hear God in a noisy, distracted life. Set apart time to search and listen.

  4. Ignorance is Costly, and Time is Precious
    Hosea 4:6—“My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge.”

    What you don’t know about God’s will can destroy your potential. Delays happen when you postpone seeking His plan.

  5. The Reward of Diligent Search is Divine Direction
    Jeremiah 29:13—“You will seek Me and find Me when you search with all your heart.”

    God does not hide His will from seekers. The more intentional your pursuit, the clearer His direction becomes.

DAILY TAKEAWAY

God’s will is not reserved for a few; it is available to all who seek it diligently. Your destiny depends on your pursuit.

SUMMARY

Knowing God’s will is your responsibility, not God’s obligation. Pursue it through Scripture, prayer, and separation until it becomes clear.

PRAYER

Father, ignite in me a hunger to know Your will. Deliver me from spiritual laziness and distractions. Guide me into the fullness of Your purpose for my life. Amen.

DAILY BIBLE READING

Ephesians 5:17, Colossians 1:9-10, Galatians 1:15-18.
