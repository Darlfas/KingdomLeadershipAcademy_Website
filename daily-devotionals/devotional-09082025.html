CHRIST IS THE REAL FOUNDATION (Part 7)

Sub-Series Focus: The Danger of Mixing Law With Grace: Why Christ Alone Must Be the Bedrock

TEXTS: Galatians 1:6–9, Galatians 2:11–16, Romans 10:4, Acts 15:5–11, James 3:1–2, Hebrews 13:9, Ephesians 2:8–9, Colossians 2:14–17

KEY SCRIPTURE

“I marvel that ye are so soon removed from him that called you into the grace of Christ unto another gospel.”

Galatians 1:6 (KJV)

INTRODUCTION

From the first century until now, the enemy’s most subtle strategy has been mixture. He knows that an outright rejection of Christ won’t easily work in the Church, so he attempts to dilute the Gospel—blending law with grace, works with faith, Moses with Christ. This is the “another gospel” that made Paul marvel and burn with holy jealousy for the purity of the faith. When law and grace are mixed, the result is not balance—it is bondage. The Law was given to Israel for a specific purpose and season; Grace was given through Christ to the world for all time. To combine them is to confuse the covenant, corrupt the message, and cripple the believer’s walk. The early Church faced it, Paul confronted it, and God is warning us again in these last days: Do not build on a split foundation. Christ alone is enough.

TODAY’S LESSON (5-Point Summary)

  1. Mixture Is Another Gospel
    Adding law to grace is not an enhancement—it’s a replacement.

    The moment you mix, you move away from Christ.

  2. The Law Ended at the Cross
    Romans 10:4—Christ is the end of the law for righteousness.

    When the curtain tore, the old covenant closed.

  3. Mixture Produces Double-Minded Believers
    One day you rest in grace, the next you fear the curse.

    The foundation shifts, and faith collapses.

  4. False Foundations Bring Stricter Judgment
    James 3:1 warns teachers—they will be judged more severely for corrupting the Gospel.

    God’s standard for truth in His pulpit has not changed.

  5. Submit to God’s Ordained Voices in This Hour
    In the countdown to the King’s return, the Spirit is raising pure voices—apostolic and prophetic fathers who guard the foundation of Christ.

    Sit under grace-filled authority, not law-driven manipulation.

DAILY TAKEAWAY (SUMMARY)

The danger of mixture is not that it makes the Gospel weaker—it makes it powerless. Christ plus anything is nothing. The apostles fought this battle in the first century; we must fight it now in the last. If the foundation is not Christ alone, the building will not survive the shaking that is coming. Grace is not a suggestion—it is the only legal operating system of the New Covenant. To step back under law is to step out of Christ.

PRAYER

Father, deliver me from every false foundation. I renounce every mixture of law and grace in my belief system. Open my eyes to see Christ as my only righteousness, my only covenant, my only hope. I submit to the authority of Your Spirit and the voices You have ordained for this generation. Keep me anchored in the unshakable foundation of grace until the appearing of my King. In Jesus’ Name, Amen.

DAILY BIBLE READING

Galatians 1:6–9, Galatians 2:11–16, Romans 10:4, Acts 15:5–11, James 3:1–2, Hebrews 13:9, Ephesians 2:8–9, Colossians 2:14–17.
